Harold Davidson

To the guy that invented zero, thanks for nothing.

My early life: I was born in the late 1980's in a small country hospital in central Illinois. I grew up in a town where everyone was a farmer or factory worker. We didn't have any programmers or businesses that even needed them. My school had two technology courses, Office Applications and Visual Basic. After high school I worked retail for 7 years while attending a local community college for a different major every semester. I knew that wasn't what I wanted to do and I had always liked computers and video games. It was time for a change.

My journey into IT: In 2014, I got on at a dental, medical, and veterinary sales company as a Help Desk Tier I. Over the next 18 months I moved through the ranks and eventually became a Senior Specialist with a specialty is procedure and security. I worked with the security and development teams to implement policies and procedures, but it still wasn't enough. I wanted to make my own apps. A friend of mine had shown me web development and it was love at first site (pun intended). I saw an ad for KU's Coding Program and I knew I had to take it. I got into the program and I finally moved out of my tiny town in Illinois to an apartment in Kansas City, MO where I live alone and spend all day in front of my computer either working from home, doing classwork, or watching Netflix if time allows.
